Why Security?

Here is my one example and I'll just say thank you very much... have a nice day: Possible Data breach involving 2.7 Billion Social Security Numbers
The current population of the United States is 330 million according to my search, and this goes into 2.7 billion a total of 8.2 times. So lets just assume there are 8 duplicate records for each US citizen, then it could possibly include every American's SSN.
Everybody deals with security, and security deals with everybody.
My degree was originally in Embedded Systems (Electrical Engineering) thus my destiny was and always had been a robotics designer or process engineer on a factory floor. Once I realized how much this work was based off of massive projects or contracts, I wondered about the long term feasability of it. Now I know it would be very stable work but this is the thought I had at the time.
Everybody and every company of ANY deals with security issues whether they know it or not. Its a commoners issue as well. Thats what drew me over initially. I had never considered moving over to cybersecurity pretty much the month before I decided to. Once I decided it was game over.
I also wanted to be constantly learning. Love learning. One of the biggest things about security that I didn't realize at the time, was that a core tenet of cyber is to understand the systems that you are protecting. If you don't understand them, then how can you protect them?
Thus started the journey we are on today of continually trying to broaden and deepen my understanding systems and their workings. Its an awesome journey!
